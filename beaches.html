Strandhill, Sligo

Strandhill is a vibrant seaside village and holiday resort located on the R292 – it is roughly 7 miles from Sligo town. With its wild Atlantic waves is also a very popular spot for surfers throughout the year. It's one of the most popular beaches in Sligo, there are also many surf schools throughtout strandhill for people new to surfing / etc.

Mullaghmore, Sligo

From https://www.surf-forecast.com/breaks/Mullaghmore : Mullaghmore in Sligo is a sheltered beach break that has quite reliable surf and can work at any time of the year. Ideal winds are from the southeast. Waves just as likely from local windswells as from distant groundswells and the ideal swell direction is from the northwest. The beach breaks offer lefts and rights. Good surf at all stages of the tide. When the surf is up, it can get quite busy in the water.
Mullaghmore can be one of the best surfing spots in the world but is also very dangerous due to the shallow reef as well as other factors. The biggest waves in Ireland occur here, so it is recommended usually only for professionals
